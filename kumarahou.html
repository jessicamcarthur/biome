Kūmarahou

Soap

Meet Kūmarahou

Kūmarahou (also known as "gumdigger's soap") is one of Aotearoa's most loved native plants when it comes to skin care.

Why It's Good For You

Kūmarahou contains natural compounds called saponins. These have natural antibacterial properties that support healing for conditions like eczema, acne, and minor rashes.

How To Make It

What You'll Need:

  • 1 cup olive oil
  • 140 ml water
  • 170 grams sodium hydroxide
  • A handful of Kūmarahou flowers

Let's Make It:

  1. Carefully mix the sodium hydroxide into the water. It'll heat up quickly, so set it aside and let it cool.
  2. Gently heat your olive oil to around 38°C
  3. Slowly pour the cooled mixture into the oil. Using a stick blender, mix until it reaches a thin trace
  4. Stir in your Kūmarahou flowers and keep blending until you reach a medium trace
  5. Pour the mixture into your moulds and leave it to harden for 24 hours.
